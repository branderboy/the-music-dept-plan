The Five Common Leaks What we'd patch first at Chicho's

Slow Tuesdays and Wednesdays

Sales drop 25 to 40% on those days, and nothing is firing to bring people in. This alone usually accounts for $8k to $15k a month.

Hidden From Google Search

Most local restaurant sites are missing the basic schema markup Google needs. Result: low impressions on the queries that should be free wins ("rotisserie chicken near me", "Peruvian Hyattsville", "catering DMV").

Google Business Profile Set to "Restaurant"

Generic primary category. Out of about 4,000 categories Google offers, 5 to 9 specific ones are sitting unused. This hides you from 40 to 60% of nearby searches.

Mismatched NAP Across Directories

Name, address, and phone don't match across 30+ directories that list local restaurants. Google reads that as a trust signal and pushes the listing down in local pack results.

Catering Pipeline With No Follow-Up

People ask about catering, nobody calls back. Office lunches, churches, schools, HOAs, every showcase typically books 5 to 15 recurring accounts. That's the pipeline most independents leave dry.

Pillar 2 · Conversion

Turn Local Demand Into Walk-Ins, Catering Books, and Captures

Strategy: Activate people in your radius with sharp offers and capture every customer's phone and email, every time, on every channel.

Deliverables

  1. Geofenced Activation AdsFacebook and Instagram ads drawn around a tight radius (delivery zone, office parks, churches, apartments, schools) with a "come in this week" offer designed to put feet on the floor.
  2. Custom Audience ScrapePull lists of people who engage with similar food pages in your area, build them into Meta custom audiences. Ads stop blasting strangers and start hitting people who already love this kind of food.
  3. Happy Hour, The Signature Play SignatureMost restaurants run promos. The good ones run events. Happy Hour is how I turn any moment into a branded event, a slow Tuesday, a holiday weekend, a sauce praised in reviews, a new menu drop. The reason it matters: a "Tuesday special" gets ignored. A named, dated event with a window unlocks free placement on platforms a promo can't touch, GMB Posts, Eventbrite, Luma, and Facebook Events all index events, not specials. We give the moment a name, list it everywhere event platforms reach, and capture phone and email on every RSVP.
    9 channels, one click. GMB Posts, Eventbrite, Luma, FB Events, Nextdoor, email, SMS, geofence ads, in-store signage.
    Every RSVP = a capture. Phone and email feed the customer list and welcome series.
    Every event mints content. Photos and short videos get reused across Reels, TikTok, and ads.
    The list compounds. Every Happy Hour adds to the list. Six months in, the list is your biggest marketing asset.
  4. Catering Showcase (Quarterly)Invite-only tasting for businesses, churches, schools, and HOAs within 5 miles. One showcase typically books 5 to 15 recurring catering accounts. Highest-ROI event we run.
  5. Monthly Text-to-Win GiveawayRecurring monthly drawing (free family meal, catering tray, gift card) promoted on table tents, receipts, food-app inserts, social posts, and ads. Captures phone and email in one move and feeds the welcome series.
  6. Third-Party Margin DefenseDoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub compared on ROAS, AOV, and conversion. Photo refreshes, item rewrites, sponsored listing optimization. Direct-order inserts in every order migrate customers off the platforms onto your own ordering link.

Pillar 3 · Retention & Intelligence

Bring Customers Back On Autopilot, Run The Restaurant From One Screen

Strategy: Connect Toast, email, SMS, and rewards into one dashboard, then turn that data into a weekly playbook and daily reminders so nothing gets missed.

Deliverables

  1. Toast as the Truth LayerDaily sales, daypart, channel mix, AOV, and email captures from Toast all flow into the dashboard. Every other asset is reconciled against this single source of truth.
  2. Lifecycle Email AutomationsWelcome series, post-purchase plus review request, birthday week free dessert, 60-day win-back with a free side, 90-day deep win-back with a free entrée, monthly VIP offer for top spenders, and catering follow-up at 90 days post-event. All running in the background.
  3. Rewards That Actually RetainToast Loyalty as the foundation. Member AOV tracked against non-member. Inactive members get auto-fired win-back triggers. Reward redemption data tells us what people actually value.
  4. Weekly Playbook (Every Sunday)A 1-page plan emailed Sunday night with the week's revenue goal, busy-day forecast, the posts and offers to run, the catering list to call, and a short note on what worked last week.
  5. Daily Reminders (Every Morning)A short text or email each morning with today's top 3 actions, who to call back, what to post, when to post it, and how much chicken to prep based on the forecast.
  6. Forecast-Driven OperationsNext-week sales predictions by day. Prep tightens. Staffing tightens. Saves 3 to 8% on labor in 90 days without touching service quality.
