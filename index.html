01 // The Business Strategy

Four Revenue Engines

The plan runs on four offers. Each one is priced, targeted, and sold to a specific buyer.

Engine 1

Lockout Days

$250/day × 14 = $3,500

Full-day rehearsal buyouts. Over 40% of the goal, sold to the buyers who book in bulk: touring bands, producers, managers.

Engine 2

Monthly Artist Blocks

$600/mo × 4 = $2,400

Eight recording hours a month, recurring. Turns recording from a one-off transaction into subscription income.

Engine 3

Podcast Residencies

$400/mo × 3 = $1,200

A weekly slot, same day, same room. Podcasts are the ideal tenant: they book 52 weeks a year and never churn mid-season.

Engine 4

Listening Parties

$650 × 2 = $1,300

A four-hour evening event with PA and engineer, sold per release to artists and labels.

Why This Works

  • 1Recurring revenue changes the business. A studio that starts every month at $3,600 makes better decisions than one that starts at zero. It funds marketing, smooths slow weeks, and makes the business sellable.
  • 2Relationships beat transactions. One producer with a weekly residency is worth more than twenty individual bookings — and costs nothing to re-acquire each month.
  • 3The buyer matters more than the booking. Managers, labels, and podcast companies buy in bulk, pay on one invoice, and sign for months. Individual artists fill the calendar; these buyers build the business.

03 // Website Strategy

The Sales Platform

The website is not a brochure. Its job is to shorten every buying decision: a buyer should land, understand the offer, see the price, and book — in under three minutes.

Offer Pages (4)

One page per revenue engine: Lockout, Artist Block, Podcast Slot, Listening Party. Each shows the rate, the gear, the room, the photos — and links directly into the existing booking system.

Audience Pages

Dedicated pages for producers, managers, labels, podcast networks, bands, and artists. Each states the deal, the math versus retail rates, and who to call. These buyers skim; the pages are built for skimming.

Capability PDF

A one-page document — rooms, gear, rates, capacity, load-in — generated from the same data as the site. Managers and labels forward files, not homepages. This is the asset that travels through their inbox.

Transparent Pricing + Direct Booking

Every rate published. Hidden pricing creates phone calls; published pricing creates bookings. Every page ends in one action: book the room.
